Numista

Notes: Owing to lack of coins at Cuba Island, provided by American minthouses (mainly Mexico), many coins of Spanish 2 Reales (colloquially called "Sevillanas") were illegally introduced. They had a lower value (American "Columnarias"= 1/4 Peso, and 2 Reales Spain= 1/5 Peso), so Spanish government decided to collect those spanish coins, but in the meanwhile, to avoid scarcity of cash, they decided to countermark to give them their real value; 50 dies were used, 20 at La Habana, 15 at Puerto Príncipe and 15 at Santigo de Cuba. Countermark on Spanish (Madrid) 2 Reales, KM#412.1.
